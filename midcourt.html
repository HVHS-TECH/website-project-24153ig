Postion and role on court

Where position can go on court

Drill

Wing Attack

The Wing Attack is in charge of feeding the ball into the shooting circle and helping move the ball down the court. They go in the center and attacking third but can not enter the shooting circle. They need to have good timing and good acctrate passes.

6 feeders WA endurance & pressure drill

How to do this drill

  • Six feeders stand on the outside of the third
  • WA starts in the third and runs to receive passes from the feeders around the third
  • WA receives 5 passes in total with the feeders
  • After completing the 5 passes, 1 defender is added to defend the WA inside the third
  • The WA does another 5 passes with the defender
  • Once completed again a second defender is added, and the WA repeats the drill
  • This continues until there are 5 defenders defending the WA while they still try to do the 5 passes

This drill is really good for WA because its good for fitness andpassing accuracy. It also teaches them how to create space, and keep control of the ball even with multiple defenders.

Center

The center is the main link between the defence and attack and is involved in almost every play. They can go in every area of the court except the shooting circles. The center supports teammates and need to be fit and have accerate passes.

Centre escape and drive drill

How to do this drill

  • At both ends of the third there are feeders one with a ball
  • The Centre starts in the middle of the third with two defenders marking them
  • The Centre must try to get free from the defenders by changing direction and creating space
  • Once free, the Centre drives to one of the feeders and gets a pass from a feeder and chooses which feeder to pass back to
  • The Centre must complete 10 passes in total while being defended

This drill is really good for Centres because it teaches them how to lose defenders and create space. It also improves fitness because they are moving and changing direction.

Wing Defence

The wing defences role is to stop the opposing wing attack from getting the ball. They work in the center and defending thrids. Their main role is to mark their player. They need good footwork and communication

Figure 8 intercept drill

How to do this drill

  • Two feeders stand a few metres apart and constantly pass the ball to each other
  • The WD moves around them in a figure 8
  • As the ball is being passed between the feeders, the WD tries to read the pass and intercept it
  • The feeders should keep the passes consistent
  • The drill continues until the WD has intercepted 20 passes, then rotate players

This drill is really good for WD because it improves their timing and ability to read the play. It helps them learn how to anticipate passes and position themselves to intercept the ball during a centre pass or general play.
